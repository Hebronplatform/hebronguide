## Preamble

World Relief (hereinafter "WR") and Hebron Platform LLC (hereinafter "HebronGuide") enter into this Memorandum of Understanding to jointly serve Korean-American immigrants through complementary strengths.

**World Relief** provides on-the-ground refugee resettlement, immigration legal services, ESL, economic empowerment, and church-based community support across the United States.

**HebronGuide** provides a Korean-language digital settlement platform connecting Korean immigrants with practical information, Korean-American community resources, and church connections in their new cities.

Both organizations share the conviction of Matthew 25:35: *"I was a stranger and you welcomed me."*

This partnership creates a seamless handoff: World Relief welcomes immigrants physically; HebronGuide helps them take root digitally.

---

## Article 1 — Purpose

1. To extend World Relief's impact among Korean-speaking immigrants by providing them a dedicated digital settlement resource.
2. To connect Korean immigrants served by World Relief with Korean-American churches and community networks.
3. To establish a referral and service partnership that benefits Korean immigrant communities across the United States.

---

## Article 2 — World Relief Commitments

### 2-1. Referral & Integration
- Distribute HebronGuide QR codes and materials to Korean-speaking immigrant clients
- Include HebronGuide in the standard Korean-language welcome packet
- Refer Korean immigrants seeking church connections to HebronGuide's church matching service
- Introduce HebronGuide to World Relief's partner church network as a Korean community resource

### 2-2. Financial Support
World Relief agrees to provide the following financial support to HebronGuide in recognition of digital platform services for Korean immigrant integration:

| Item | Amount | Schedule |
|---|---|---|
| Annual Digital Services Fee | $10,000 | Upon signing |
| Optional: Grant for Korean Content Development | $5,000–$15,000 | Per grant cycle |
| Optional: Program Co-funding (pilot) | TBD | Per program |

> Note: This fee structure reflects HebronGuide's service as World Relief's Korean-language digital integration partner. Subject to negotiation and World Relief's funding availability.

### 2-3. Institutional Support
- Endorse HebronGuide in World Relief's communications to partner churches and stakeholders
- Explore joint grant applications to foundations funding immigrant integration programs
- Provide World Relief's institutional credibility for HebronGuide's nonprofit development efforts

---

## Article 3 — HebronGuide Commitments

### 3-1. Digital Services
- Maintain and develop Korean-language settlement content covering: housing, healthcare, legal services, DMV, employment, education, banking, and church connections
- Provide Korean-language airport arrival and city orientation guides
- Connect Korean immigrants with World Relief's local offices where available

### 3-2. Church Connection
- Facilitate connections between Korean immigrants and Korean churches through HebronGuide's church matching service
- Equip HebronGuide-listed churches to serve World Relief's Korean immigrant clients

### 3-3. Data & Reporting
- Provide quarterly usage reports to World Relief: Korean-language users, city breakdown, church connections made
- Co-present partnership impact data at relevant conferences or stakeholder meetings

### 3-4. Branding
- Display "World Relief Partner" designation on HebronGuide where appropriate
- Reference World Relief in relevant settlement content as a trusted resource

---

## Article 4 — Pilot Program (Recommended First Step)

Before full-scale partnership, both parties recommend a **6-month pilot program** in Seattle:

**Goal:** Serve 50 newly arrived Korean immigrants in Greater Seattle  
**Method:**
- World Relief Seattle office distributes HebronGuide QR to Korean clients
- HebronGuide tracks engagement and church connections made
- Joint debrief at 3 months and 6 months

**Pilot Budget:** $2,000 (from World Relief, covering HebronGuide content localization for pilot)

---

## Article 5 — Term & Renewal

- Term: One (1) year from signing
- Auto-renewal: Unless either party provides written notice 30 days before expiration
- Annual review meeting required before renewal

---

## Article 6 — Intellectual Property

Each party retains ownership of its own content, data, and materials. Joint content requires written agreement specifying ownership and usage rights.

---

## Article 7 — Confidentiality

Both parties agree to protect the confidentiality of client data, financial information, and operational details shared under this agreement, in compliance with applicable privacy laws.

---

## Article 8 — Termination

Either party may terminate with 30 days written notice, or immediately for material breach. Prepaid fees will be refunded on a pro-rata basis.
